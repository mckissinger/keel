# Content types — the four the verb covers

One pipeline, four framings. The content type is a Discover-phase question decided
in the video brief; it sets the script source, the framing, and the target length.
Lengths are guidance ranges, not gates — the script's job decides, the range keeps
it honest.

## Feature how-to / knowledge-base video (the core case)

- **Script source:** that feature's spec — its screens, enumerated states, and
  primary flow. One feature, one video.
- **Framing:** task-first. Open on the outcome ("here's the report you'll have"),
  then the steps that reach it. No product preamble — the viewer already owns the
  product and arrived with a task.
- **Length:** 60–180 seconds; roughly 5–12 driven steps. A feature that genuinely
  needs more is usually two videos (two tasks), not one longer one.

## Onboarding sequence

- **Script source:** the product skeleton's **primary flows** — the path a new
  user walks from first sign-in to first value. A series over the skeleton, not a
  single video: each episode covers one flow.
- **Framing:** journey-first. Each episode opens with where the viewer is in the
  journey ("you've connected your data; now let's make it visible") and closes by
  naming the next episode's step.
- **Length:** 45–120 seconds per episode; 3–6 episodes for a typical skeleton.
  Shorter episodes beat one long tour — a new user watches between attempts.

## Marketing demo reel

- **Script source:** the marketing surface's own claims — the feature bands and
  proof points the `marketing-site` plan committed — walked against the real app.
- **Framing:** claim-then-proof. Each scene shows one marketed capability actually
  happening; the reel **composes with the marketing surface and its assets — it
  never redesigns them** (that surface, tokens and all, is `marketing-site`'s to
  own). Pacing is faster than education content: cut on the action, not the
  explanation.
- **Length:** 30–90 seconds total. A reel that wants to explain is a how-to
  wearing the wrong clothes — split it.

## Companion step-doc

- **Script source:** **the same scenes manifest as its video — never authored
  separately.** Each scene's narration text becomes the step's prose; each driven
  step becomes the step's instruction; captured frames become the step's
  screenshots.
- **Framing:** a markdown step-by-step guide, one numbered step per scene, suitable
  for a knowledge base beside (or instead of) the video for readers who scan.
- **Length:** exactly the video's scene count — the doc and the video never drift
  because they share one source of truth.
